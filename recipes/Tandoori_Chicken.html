Tandoori Chicken

What is Tandoori Chicken ?

Tandoori chicken is a popular Indian dish known for its flavorful and aromatic preparation. It involves marinating chicken pieces in a mixture of yogurt and various spices, and then cooking them in a tandoor, which is a traditional clay oven. The result is juicy and tender chicken with a distinctive smoky flavor.

Ingredients You need to make Tandoori Chicken ü§î

  • 1.5 kg (3.3 lbs) chicken, cut into pieces
  • 1 cup plain yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 tablespoons tandoori masala (available in most grocery stores)
  • 1 tablespoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • 1 tablespoon turmeric powder
  • 1 tablespoon garam masala
  • 1 tablespoon cumin powder
  • 1 tablespoon coriander powder
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • Fresh coriander leaves and lemon wedges for garnish

How to make Tandoori Chicken üçó

  1. Marination:
    • In a large bowl, mix yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, tandoori masala, red chili powder, turmeric powder, garam masala, cumin powder, coriander powder, salt, lemon juice, and vegetable oil to form a smooth marinade.
  2. Preparation of Chicken:
    • Make deep cuts in the chicken pieces to allow the marinade to penetrate.
    • Coat the chicken pieces evenly with the marinade, making sure to get the mixture into the cuts.
    • Cover the bowl and let the chicken marinate for at least 2-4 hours, or preferably overnight in the refrigerator.The longer the marination, the more flavorful the chicken will be.
  3. Cooking:
    • Preheat your oven to the highest temperature or use a grill if you have one.
    • If using an oven, place a rack on a baking sheet and arrange the marinated chicken pieces on the rack.
    • Cook in the preheated oven or grill for about 20-25 minutes, turning occasionally, until the chicken is fully cooked and has a nice char on the outside.
  4. Garnish:
    • Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and serve with lemon wedges on the side.
